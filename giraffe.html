Giraffe

A Giraffe is an African mammal and the tallest living native animal and the largest ruminant. The name comes from the camel-like appearance and the patches of colour on its fur. The Giraffes characteristics are its extremely long neck and legs, its distinctive coat pattern. Giraffes eat leaves which they search for at heights most other herbivores cannot reach [2]. Giraffes are preyed on by lions and their calves are also preyed on by leopards and hyenas.
